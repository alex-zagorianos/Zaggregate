# Research — LinkedIn access + public-scraping legality (2026) (2026-07-01)

_Confidence: high — core legal claims (hiQ final judgment, Meta v Bright Data holding, Van Buren holding, DOJ policy, Proxycurl/ProAPIs shutdowns) are each corroborated by 2+ independent sources; robots.txt and User Agreement text were fetched live and directly (not inferred) on 2026-07-01, and cross-checked against JobScout's actual source files (config.py, linkedin_guest_client.py, browser_ext/manifest.json, brain/scraping-sources.md)._

## Summary
LinkedIn's `jobs-guest` HTML endpoint (used by JobScout's `search/linkedin_guest_client.py`) still returns data as of mid-2026, but LinkedIn's live `robots.txt` explicitly disallows it (`Disallow: /jobs-guest/` for every named crawler, and a catch-all `User-agent: * / Disallow: /` that covers JobScout's spoofed Chrome UA), and LinkedIn's User Agreement §1.2 states the contract "applies to Members and Visitors" — i.e. it's drafted specifically to bind unauthenticated guest traffic, closing the "logged-off scraping isn't ToS-bound" loophole that Bright Data won against Meta. CFAA/criminal risk is genuinely low post-*Van Buren* (2021) and the 9th Circuit's 2022 *hiQ* remand (no "gates down" on a page anyone can load), reinforced by DOJ's 2022 charging policy that will not prosecute pure ToS violations against public web services. But civil breach-of-contract risk is real and precedented (*hiQ* itself lost on contract even while winning on CFAA), and 2025-2026 LinkedIn enforcement has been aggressive against commercial resellers: Proxycurl was sued Jan-2025 and shut down permanently Jul-4-2025; ProAPIs was sued Oct-2025 and settled in 2026. Proxycurl's replacement never appeared — there is no surviving cheap third-party LinkedIn jobs API, and LinkedIn's own Job Posting API is outbound-only (employers→LinkedIn, partner-gated, closed to new AWLI partners since Oct-2025). The defensible, already-partially-built path for JobScout is the existing `browser_ext/` "Job Harvester" extension — user-gated, single-page, DOM-read capture of a job posting the human already opened — which is the same model Teal/Jobscan/Huntr use commercially without incident.

## Findings
## 1. Is `linkedin_guest_client.py`'s approach still working, and how blocked is it?

JobScout's [`search/linkedin_guest_client.py`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\search\linkedin_guest_client.py) hits `https://www.linkedin.com/jobs-guest/jobs/api/seeMoreJobPostings/search` (config: [`config.py:257`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\config.py:257)) unauthenticated, with a spoofed desktop-Chrome user-agent string and a sliding-window limiter of **3 requests/minute** (`config.py:258`, ~1 request per 20s — see `search/http_util.py:34` `RateLimiter`, which is per-minute not per-second). It's off by default, opt-in only via `--sources linkedin_guest` (module docstring, `linkedin_guest_client.py:1-6`).

- **Functionally**: this public HTML-fragment endpoint (no auth, no cookies) still works in 2026 — multiple current (2026-dated) scraping-vendor blog posts confirm it returns paginated job cards via `start=` offset with no login wall. [scrapfly.io](https://scrapfly.io/blog/posts/how-to-scrape-linkedin), [dev.to (agenthustler)](https://dev.to/agenthustler/how-to-scrape-linkedin-job-listings-in-2026-python-public-api-no-login-required-5bin).
- **How aggressively blocked**: not IP-banned outright at low volume, but industry consensus is it's fragile — "a working scraper breaks every 4 to 8 weeks when LinkedIn ships a defense update," with rate limiting, browser fingerprinting, and CAPTCHA challenges kicking in at scale ([iproyal.com](https://iproyal.com/blog/web-scraping-linkedin/), [generect.com](https://generect.com/blog/linkedin-scraping/)). JobScout's 3/min limiter is well under any commercial-scraper concern, but there's no SLA — it can be silently broken by a markup/endpoint change at any time (already anticipated in JobScout's own cache/fallback design).
- **robots.txt (verified live, fetched directly 2026-07-01)**: `https://www.linkedin.com/robots.txt` explicitly lists `Disallow: /jobs-guest/` under every *named* crawler group (Googlebot, Bingbot, Applebot, the Claude-family bots, GPTBot, etc.) **and** ends with a catch-all:
  ```
  User-agent: *
  Disallow: /
  ```
  Any UA string not explicitly named — which includes JobScout's spoofed `Chrome/124.0 Safari/537.36` string — falls under this blanket disallow of the entire site. This is new, concrete evidence not currently reflected in JobScout's own `brain/scraping-sources.md:43` note ("Actively blocks, ToS risk").
- **Crawling Terms** (`linkedin.com/legal/crawling-terms`, linked from the robots.txt header comment): "Automated Crawling & Indexing without the express permission of LinkedIn is strictly prohibited," requires emailing `whitelist-crawl@linkedin.com` for permission, and explicitly requires bots to "use their own true IP address and useragent identity" — i.e. UA-spoofing (which `linkedin_guest_client.py:21-22` does) is itself named as a violation of LinkedIn's own crawling policy, independent of the scraping question.
- **User Agreement §8.2 ("Don'ts")**: prohibits developing/using "software, devices, scripts, robots... to scrape or copy the Services" and "bots or other unauthorized automated methods to access the Services." Critically, **§1.2 states "This Contract applies to Members and Visitors"** and §1.1 triggers the binding contract on "accessing or using our Services" — not just registering. This is a deliberate contractual design to bind unauthenticated/guest traffic, which matters directly for the *Meta v. Bright Data* comparison below.

**Bottom line on (1):** technically functional, but LinkedIn's written policies (robots.txt, crawling terms, User Agreement) unambiguously prohibit it — this is a documented civil/contract exposure, not a hidden gray area.

## 2. Alternatives

**Proxycurl — dead.** LinkedIn sued Nubela Pte Ltd / Proxycurl LLC / founder Steven Goh in the Northern District of California, Jan 24 2025 (Case No. 3:25-cv-00828), alleging the service ran "hundreds of thousands" of fake accounts to scrape millions of profiles at scale. Rather than fight a multi-billion-dollar litigant, Proxycurl shut down permanently on **July 4, 2025**; the settlement/injunction requires permanent deletion of all LinkedIn-derived data. [nubela.co founder post](https://nubela.co/blog/goodbye-proxycurl/), [startuphub.ai](https://www.startuphub.ai/ai-news/startup-news/2025/the-1-linkedin-scraping-startup-proxycurl-shuts-down), [socialmediatoday.com](https://www.socialmediatoday.com/news/linkedin-wins-legal-case-data-scrapers-proxycurl/756101/). There is no comparable successor cheap/legal LinkedIn-jobs API today.

**ProAPIs — also sued, also folded.** LinkedIn sued ProAPIs Oct 2 2025 (N.D. Cal.) for the same fake-account-at-scale pattern; the parties reached an "agreement in principle" to settle in 2026. [thelinkedblog.com](https://thelinkedblog.com/2026/linkedin-reaches-deal-in-data-scraping-lawsuit-against-proapis-3857/). Pattern: LinkedIn is systematically litigating every commercial-scale reseller that uses fake/automated accounts — a fact pattern that is *categorically different* from JobScout's no-account, rate-limited, opt-in personal tool, but confirms there's no safe commercial substitute to buy instead.

**Official LinkedIn Job Posting API / Talent Solutions — wrong direction, and closed.** This API family (`developer.linkedin.com/product-catalog/talent`, [Microsoft Learn docs](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/linkedin/talent/job-postings/api/overview?view=li-lts-2026-03)) lets *employers/ATSs* push job postings **onto** LinkedIn — it is not a read API for consuming LinkedIn's job inventory into a third-party seeker tool, is partner-gated with a signed data-restricted agreement, and as of **October 1, 2025 LinkedIn stopped accepting new partners** for the adjacent "Apply with LinkedIn" program entirely. Not a viable path for JobScout regardless of legality.

**JSearch (RapidAPI)** — JobScout already uses this (`brain/scraping-sources.md:17-26`, config gated, 200 req/month free tier). It aggregates Google-for-Jobs' structured `JobPosting` JSON-LD feed, which *indirectly* includes some LinkedIn-originated postings (among many boards), but it is not a direct LinkedIn integration and its LinkedIn coverage is neither guaranteed nor LinkedIn-endorsed — treat as "some incidental LinkedIn coverage," not a LinkedIn substitute.

**User-gated browser-extension capture — the compliant path, and already built.** JobScout's [`browser_ext/manifest.json`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\browser_ext\manifest.json) ("Job Harvester" v1.4) already implements this correctly: `host_permissions`/`content_scripts` scoped only to job-detail pages (`linkedin.com/jobs/*`, plus Indeed/Glassdoor/ZipRecruiter/Dice), `activeTab` permission only, content script fires on `document_idle` — i.e. it reads the DOM of a page the human already opened in their own browser session, with no separate bot account, no bulk crawling, no background polling. This mirrors exactly what commercially-successful, unsued job-tracker extensions do today: Teal ("save jobs from 40+ boards... click the icon while viewing a listing"), Jobscan, Huntr, JobHunnt ([tealhq.com](https://www.tealhq.com/tool/job-search-chrome-extension), [Jobscan Chrome listing](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/jobscan-job-search-tracke/apppjobnbahbomhgmcgolplkpigjlofl)). It is **not** zero-risk under a hyper-literal reading of LinkedIn's Don't #2 ("software... to scrape or copy... data from the Services"), but it is the overwhelmingly-adopted, low-enforcement-priority pattern in the entire job-search-tool category, and functionally indistinguishable from a user manually copy-pasting — the opposite fact pattern from Proxycurl/ProAPIs (fake accounts, bulk, no user in the loop).

## 3. Legal landscape — final state of the cited cases + 2025-2026 developments

- **Van Buren v. United States (SCOTUS, 2021)**: narrowed CFAA's "exceeds authorized access" to a technical "gates-up-or-down" test — accessing information you're technically permitted to reach (even for a purpose that violates a contract/ToS) is not a CFAA violation. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Van_Buren_v._United_States), [Proskauer](https://newmedialaw.proskauer.com/2021/06/06/supreme-court-ends-long-running-circuit-split-over-cfaa-exceeds-authorized-access-issue-adopting-a-narrow-interpretation-that-will-reverberate-in-scraping-disputes-and-litigation-ov/).
- **hiQ Labs v. LinkedIn — FINAL STATE**: SCOTUS GVR'd the 9th Circuit's original 2019 pro-hiQ CFAA ruling in light of *Van Buren* (June 2021); on remand the **9th Circuit reaffirmed (April 18, 2022)** that scraping a page with no access controls "cannot violate the CFAA" ([Proskauer](https://www.proskauer.com/blog/taking-cue-from-the-supreme-courts-van-buren-decision-ninth-circuit-releases-new-opinion-holding-scraping-of-publicly-available-website-data-falls-outside-of-cfaa)). BUT on the merits back in district court, a **Nov 4, 2022 summary judgment found hiQ breached LinkedIn's User Agreement** — both directly, and via hiQ's "turkers" who created fake LinkedIn accounts to QA hiQ's product (a separate CFAA-triggering fake-account/password-wall breach). The case ended in a **stipulated Dec 6/7, 2022 consent judgment: $500,000 against hiQ + a permanent injunction** ordering hiQ to stop all LinkedIn scraping and destroy all derived data/code ([Privacy World](https://www.privacyworld.blog/2022/12/linkedins-data-scraping-battle-with-hiq-labs-ends-with-proposed-judgment/), [Proskauer](https://newmedialaw.proskauer.com/2022/11/11/court-finds-hiq-breached-linkedins-terms-prohibiting-scraping-but-in-mixed-ruling-declines-to-grant-summary-judgment-to-either-party-as-to-certain-key-issues/)). **Net takeaway: CFAA is dead as a weapon against pure public scraping; breach-of-contract is very much alive, and LinkedIn already won it once.**
- **Meta Platforms v. Bright Data (N.D. Cal., Jan 23 2024 SJ; case dropped by Meta Feb 23 2024)**: Judge Chen held Facebook/Instagram's ToS "do not bar logged-off scraping of public data" because a logged-out visitor isn't bound as a contracted "user" under *Meta's specific* wording ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2024/01/24/court-rules-in-favor-of-a-web-scraper-bright-data-which-meta-had-used-and-then-sued/), [Bright Data's own writeup](https://brightdata.com/blog/web-data/court-rules-in-favor-of-bright-data-in-meta-v-bright-data-case)). **This is the case JobScout's guest-endpoint approach would want to lean on — but it likely doesn't transfer to LinkedIn**, because LinkedIn's User Agreement §1.2 explicitly says "applies to Members **and Visitors**," closing exactly the loophole Bright Data exploited against Meta. hiQ (contract claim won against public-page scraping) is the more analogous LinkedIn precedent.
- **X Corp. v. Bright Data (N.D. Cal., May 2024, Judge Alsup)**: dismissed X's breach-of-contract/ToS claims on **Copyright Act preemption** grounds — a scraper-favorable ruling, but on a narrow doctrinal theory (state contract claims over content overlapping with copyright subject matter are preempted by federal copyright law) ([Skadden](https://www.skadden.com/insights/publications/2024/05/district-court-adopts-broad-view), [ipandmedialaw.fkks.com](https://ipandmedialaw.fkks.com/post/102j7d0/blockbuster-ruling-federal-court-holds-that-copyright-act-preempts-xs-web-scrap)). Untested against LinkedIn; also weaker fit for JobScout since job-posting *metadata* (title/company/location/salary/URL — the only fields `linkedin_guest_client.py:56-68` extracts) is factual, not copyrightable expression, so this preemption shield is unlikely to apply.
- **DOJ CFAA charging policy (May 19, 2022, still current)**: explicitly states DOJ will **not** prosecute CFAA cases resting solely on a ToS/contract violation against a public web service — federal criminal exposure for this kind of scraping is essentially nil in good faith. [DOJ press release](https://www.justice.gov/archives/opa/pr/department-justice-announces-new-policy-charging-cases-under-computer-fraud-and-abuse-act).
- **2025-2026 LinkedIn enforcement pattern**: every LinkedIn scraping suit found (Proxycurl Jan-2025, ProAPIs Oct-2025) targets **commercial, at-scale, fake-account-based** operations — not individual hobbyist/local tools. No case found of LinkedIn suing an individual or a free local desktop app for low-volume, no-account, rate-limited personal use.
- **GDPR/CCPA**: materially lower risk for JobScout specifically because `linkedin_guest_client.py` only ever touches `/jobs-guest/` job-search results and extracts non-personal fields (title, company, location, salary range, URL, posting date) — it does not touch `/profile/` or any individual's personal data, unlike Proxycurl (which scraped people-profile PII, triggering the France DPA's €240k KASPR fine as a comparable). If JobScout ever added a LinkedIn *people/profile* scraper, that would flip this into real GDPR/CCPA exposure; job postings alone stay low.

## Posture recommendation

**Treat `linkedin_guest_client.py` as legal_risk: medium (civil/contract), not "low."** CFAA/criminal exposure is genuinely near-zero (Van Buren + hiQ-remand + DOJ policy all agree). But LinkedIn's own robots.txt (`Disallow: /jobs-guest/` + catch-all `Disallow: /`), Crawling Terms (explicit UA-spoofing prohibition), and User Agreement (`§1.2` binds "Visitors," `§8.2` bans bots/scraping) are unambiguous, and *hiQ* proves LinkedIn can and does win breach-of-contract claims over public-page scraping. This doesn't mean remove the client — JobScout's existing design (off-by-default, `--sources linkedin_guest` opt-in, 3 req/min limiter, no login/account, no bulk resale) is about as low-risk as an automated LinkedIn client can be, and is a world away from the Proxycurl/ProAPIs fact pattern LinkedIn actually litigates against. It means **documenting the risk accurately** rather than the current soft "Actively blocks, ToS risk" note in `brain/scraping-sources.md:43`, and **not scaling it up** (no proxy rotation, no Scrapling-stealth bypass specifically for LinkedIn — `brain/scraping-sources.md:89` already has this instinct right: "ToS still applies... LinkedIn stays low-volume/last-resort").

**Make the browser extension the primary/preferred LinkedIn channel, not a side feature.** `browser_ext/` already implements the industry-standard compliant pattern (Teal/Jobscan/Huntr-equivalent). It has zero robots.txt exposure (it's not a bot request at all — it's the user's own browser rendering the page, and the extension just reads the resulting DOM), no rate-limit/block risk, and a materially better legal footing than any server-side scraper. Expanding its LinkedIn coverage (e.g. capturing the *search-results* page, not just individual `/jobs/*` postings) would reduce reliance on the guest endpoint entirely.

**Do not chase a LinkedIn data API.** Proxycurl is gone and its team didn't rebuild a competitor; ProAPIs just got sued into a settlement; LinkedIn's own Job Posting API is outbound/partner-only and closed to new entrants since Oct-2025. No free/cheap/legal bulk LinkedIn-jobs API exists in 2026 — this confirms the current "guest client (opt-in, low-volume) + browser extension (user-gated)" dual approach is already the correct architecture, not a placeholder waiting for a better API to appear.
