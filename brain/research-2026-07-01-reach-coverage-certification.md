# Research — Certifying 90-100% coverage (statistics) (2026-07-01)

_Confidence: Medium-high. Core statistical formulas (Chapman, Chao1/Chao2, jackknife, Good-Turing, log-linear, Chao-Jost coverage) are well-established and cross-verified against multiple sources. The one directly-on-topic academic paper for job-vacancy capture-recapture (Beresewicz et al. 2021) was only accessible via abstract + GitHub README, not full text, so its Chatterjee-Bhuyan negative-dependence formula is described qualitatively, not reproduced exactly. BLS JOLTS MSA-level availability and Census CBP API mechanics are verified from BLS/Census own pages. All file/line references were read directly from the JobScout repo._

## Summary
Capture-recapture is the right tool family for JobScout's "have we found 90-100%?" question, and the app already has real infrastructure for it (coverage/estimators.py: Chapman, a Chao-style estimator, Good-Turing, log-linear; coverage/registry_coverage.py for company-level; coverage/jolts.py for a macro sanity gate). The two hard failure modes for job data are (1) source non-independence — many aggregators re-syndicate the same underlying Indeed/Google-for-Jobs/LinkedIn feed, which silently inflates the estimated N̂ and makes coverage look better than reality — and (2) heterogeneous catchability — common titles (RN, SWE) are massively over-represented in APIs relative to niche/senior roles that live only on direct ATS pages, so one global coverage number is misleading. The concrete fix is: add Chao2 (incidence, with its (t-1)/t finite-sample correction), jackknife1/jackknife2, and bootstrap CIs to coverage/estimators.py; stratify every estimate per SOC/industry the way coverage/reference.py already stratifies its reference-proxy; explicitly group correlated sources before running capture-recapture; fix coverage/jolts.py's `_series_id()` (it ignores its own `area`/`naics` args and always queries the national series); and add a Census County Business Patterns macro gate as the company-level analogue of the JOLTS job-level gate. Surface it as "~X% (95% CI Y-Z%) of the reachable {area}/{industry} universe, ~N unseen, confidence: {n independent source families}" — never a bare percentage — falling back honestly to "cannot certify" when fewer than 2 independent sources exist, exactly as coverage/registry_coverage.py's `nan`-when-`m==0` behavior already does.

## Findings
## The toolkit and what each method assumes

**Lincoln-Petersen / Chapman.** N̂_LP = n1·n2/m (n1, n2 = sizes of two independent samples, m = overlap). Badly biased small-sample; the **Chapman (1951)** correction JobScout already uses in [`coverage/estimators.py:17-21`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\estimators.py:17) is standard best practice: N̂_C = (n1+1)(n2+1)/(m+1) − 1, Var = (n1+1)(n2+1)(n1−m)(n2−m) / [(m+1)²(m+2)]. Assumes: closed population, no linkage error, and — critically — **independence of the two capture occasions**. Break: any two web-scraped job sources that both re-syndicate Indeed/Google-for-Jobs are *not* independent captures; the resulting N̂ is biased low (looks like there's less unseen population than there is), i.e. coverage looks artificially high.

**Chao1 (abundance) vs Chao2 (incidence).** Chao1 (Chao 1984, bias-corrected 1987): Ŝ = S_obs + f1(f1−1)/(2(f2+1)), f1/f2 = singleton/doubleton *counts* in an abundance sample. Chao2 is the incidence analogue for *t* presence/absence samples (JobScout's actual shape — each source is a 0/1 capture occasion, not an abundance count): Ŝ_Chao2 = S_obs + [(t−1)/t]·Q1(Q1−1)/(2(Q2+1)), Q1/Q2 = number of entities found in exactly 1/2 of the *t* samples. [`coverage/estimators.py:23-24`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\estimators.py:23) is literally the Chao1 abundance formula but [`coverage/benchmark.py:56-58`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\benchmark.py:56) calls it with f1/f2 defined as counts of clusters seen by exactly 1/2 *sources* — that's incidence data, so it's really doing Chao2 without the `(t-1)/t` finite-sample correction and with no variance. Source: Chao species-richness literature, summarized at [uvm.edu Gotelli & Colwell ch.4](https://www.uvm.edu/~ngotelli/manuscriptpdfs/Chapter%204.pdf), [MetricGate Chao1 docs](https://metricgate.com/docs/chao-richness-estimator/).

**Jackknife (Burnham & Overton 1978/1979; Heltshe & Forrester 1983 incidence form).** First-order: Ŝ_jack1 = S_obs + Q1·(t−1)/t. Second-order: Ŝ_jack2 = S_obs + Q1(2t−3)/t − Q2(t−2)²/[t(t−1)]. Less biased than Chao2 when heterogeneity is high but samples (t) are moderate; a good cross-check estimator, not currently in `coverage/estimators.py` at all. Sources: [Burnham & Overton 1979, Ecology](https://esajournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.2307/1936861); [SPECIES R package jackknife docs](https://rdrr.io/cran/SPECIES/man/jackknife.html).

**Bootstrap (Smith & Van Belle 1984).** Resample the t capture occasions (or entities) with replacement B times, recompute the point estimator each time, take the 2.5/97.5 percentiles as the CI. This is the *only* CI available for `loglinear()` — [`coverage/estimators.py:31-56`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\estimators.py:31) currently returns a bare `float` with **no variance at all**, unlike `chapman()` which returns `var`+`ci95`. Since `loglinear()` is the path used whenever JobScout has ≥3 sources ([`coverage/benchmark.py:43-45`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\benchmark.py:43)), this is a real, load-bearing gap.

**Good-Turing sample coverage (Turing/Good 1953).** Ĉ = 1 − f1/n: the estimated fraction of the *true* population's probability mass already represented by observed classes. JobScout's [`good_turing()`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\estimators.py:26-29) implements this correctly. Note it estimates *coverage of probability mass*, not *count* — a useful complementary signal to Chao2/N̂ (count-based), and the two can legitimately disagree when catchability is heterogeneous.

**Chao & Jost (2012) coverage-based rarefaction/extrapolation; iNEXT.** Rather than comparing samples of equal *size*, standardize by equal *sample coverage* (the Good-Turing quantity above) and extrapolate a Hill-number diversity curve to a target coverage (e.g. "extrapolate to 95% coverage, read off Ŝ"). This is the most defensible way to answer "how many more sources would we need to reach 90%?" without over-trusting a single asymptotic point estimate. [Chao & Jost 2012, Ecology 93:2533-2547](https://esajournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1890/11-1952.1); implemented in the `iNEXT` R package ([CRAN](https://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/iNEXT/iNEXT.pdf), v3.0.2, updated 2025-07-30) — worth porting the coverage-extrapolation *formula*, not the R dependency.

**Log-linear models for >2 samples (Fienberg 1972, Cormack 1989).** Fit a Poisson GLM on the 2^k presence/absence contingency table and predict the "all-zero" cell (never captured) to get N̂. JobScout's [`_loglinear_glm()`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\estimators.py:44-56) does this correctly for main effects, but fits **no interaction terms** — i.e. it's the independence model M0, not a dependence-aware model. Real capture-recapture practice (Cormack 1989; recent survey [arXiv:2407.03539](https://arxiv.org/html/2407.03539) "Population Size Estimation with Many Lists and Heterogeneity") explicitly warns that omitting 2-way interactions when sources are correlated biases N̂ downward (over-optimistic coverage) — exactly JobScout's risk given aggregators that share upstream feeds.

**Reference-set overlap.** JobScout's [`coverage/reference.py`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\reference.py) already does this: pull a small paid-API "gold" sample per SOC group and measure containment (N_g/D_g). This is methodologically close to the classic **TREC pooling-bias problem** in information retrieval — relevance judgments (or here, "found" status) are only reliable for the union of what participating systems retrieved, and systems/sources that don't contribute to the pool are systematically under-credited ([Zobel 1998](https://dl.acm.org/doi/pdf/10.1145/1277741.1277755), well-documented at NIST TREC). The fix there — and here — is the same: treat the reference sample as a lower-assumption *spot check*, not a full census.

**Macro sanity gates (JOLTS + a company-level analogue).** JOLTS national job openings were 7.6M in April/May 2026 ([BLS JOLTS news release](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/jolts.nr0.htm)). The BLS API v2 requires a free registration key: 500 queries/day, 20 years of history, 50 series/query registered vs. 25/25/10 unregistered ([BD Econ BLS API guide](https://bd-econ.com/blsapi.html); [BLS API features](https://www.bls.gov/bls/api_features.htm)). **Critical bug**: [`coverage/jolts.py:47-49`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\jolts.py:47) `_series_id(area, naics)` receives both args but always returns the constant national series `"JTU000000000000000JOL"` — the JOLTS gate never actually reflects the area/industry it's supposedly gating. JOLTS *does* now publish series-code slots for state and area (restructured Oct 2020) and 2022-NAICS industry codes, but true metro-level JOLTS is explicitly labeled **"research estimates"** only, not an official series ([BLS JOLTS MSA page](https://www.bls.gov/jlt/jlt_msadata.htm)) — so sub-state gating should honestly return `"skip"`, not silently compare against the wrong denominator. For the **company-level** side there is no macro gate at all today; the free [Census County Business Patterns API](https://www.census.gov/data/developers/data-sets/cbp-zbp/cbp-api.html) (establishment counts by NAICS/county/MSA, free key, current vintage = 2023 data released June 2025) is the natural analogue — it can't identity-match individual companies, but it bounds "how many employers of this NAICS/size should exist here."

**Job-vacancy-specific capture-recapture precedent.** The closest real academic analogue is Beresewicz et al., ["Estimating the number of entities with vacancies using administrative and online data"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.03263) (2021, dual-system estimation combining Polish public-employment-office records with Pracuj.pl online postings). Key finding: the official survey **undercounted** entities-with-vacancies by ~10-15%, and — most relevant to JobScout — their two sources had *low overlap and negative dependence*, so they had to build a DSE variant for negatively-dependent sources (Chatterjee & Bhuyan 2020) rather than assume the textbook independence model. This is a direct warning that JobScout's own sources (curated registry, Common-Crawl harvest, dataset seed, enterprise-ATS discovery) should not be assumed independent by default — each pair needs an explicit justification, exactly as [`coverage/registry_coverage.py:13-19`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\registry_coverage.py:13)'s docstring already insists ("GOOD pairs... BAD pair").

## Operational design for JobScout

**Company level** (extends `coverage/registry_coverage.py`): capture occasions = (a) curated `companies.json` registry, (b) Common-Crawl ATS harvest (`discover/cc_harvest.py`), (c) `discover/dataset_seed.py` bulk import, (d) enterprise-ATS host-index discovery — 4 genuinely distinct list-building *processes*, satisfying the ≥3-source threshold for `loglinear()` rather than falling back to 2-source Chapman. Never treat the LLM metro enumerator as an independent occasion (already correctly excluded). Add Chao2 + jackknife1/jackknife2 as cross-checks; when they diverge by more than ~10-15% of each other, report "insufficient agreement to certify" rather than a single number — this mirrors Chao & Jost's own guidance to compare estimators, not trust one. Gate against Census CBP establishment counts.

**Job level** (extends `coverage/benchmark.py`): capture occasions = the aggregator/scraper source_ids already tracked in `Cluster.source_ids` ([`coverage/resolve.py:15-18`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\resolve.py:15)). Before computing membership, collapse sources known to share an upstream feed (Google-for-Jobs-backed APIs, Indeed-syndicated engines) into one meta-source via an explicit, documented independence map — the single highest-value fix, since undetected correlation is the main way this whole system silently overstates confidence. Stratify per SOC (reusing `reference.py`'s `_soc_of_cluster` grouping) since common titles vs. niche/senior titles have structurally different catchability — a global number averages over that heterogeneity and is defensible for neither group. Gate against JOLTS (fixed to use the correct area/industry series, or explicit skip for sub-state scope).

**Certification statement.** Compose from `CoverageReport` fields already defined in [`coverage/report.py`](E:\ClaudeWork\ZAG0005 - Job Search App\coverage\report.py): "You are seeing ~{cov_cr*100:.0f}% (95% CI {lo}-{hi}%) of the reachable {area}/{industry} universe; ~{N̂-observed} employers/jobs likely still unseen. Confidence: {n_independent_sources} independent source families; JOLTS gate: {verdict}." When `paths_used["cr"] == "insufficient_sources"` or the Chapman/Chao2/jackknife estimates disagree wildly, the honest answer is "cannot certify," matching the existing `CoverageEstimate.defined` / `nan`-on-zero-overlap behavior in `registry_coverage.py` rather than fabricating false precision.

**Surfacing.** Extend the "Reach dashboard" already noted as an open item (session 24 handoff) with a color-banded badge (green ≥85% + narrow CI; yellow 60-85%; red/undefined otherwise), always showing the CI and source-independence count, never a bare percentage — precisely because a single confident-looking number is the easiest way to lose a user's trust once they notice, e.g., that half the "independent" sources were re-syndicating the same feed.

## Key recommendations

- **[S/High — closes the estimator-agreement gap that lets a single point estimate masquerade as certified coverage/risk:none]** Add Chao2 (incidence-based, with the (t-1)/t finite-sample correction) and jackknife1/jackknife2 estimators to coverage/estimators.py alongside the existing Chapman/Chao1/Good-Turing/log-linear, and wire them into coverage/benchmark.py as cross-checks against the current mislabeled chao1() call (which is fed incidence f1/f2 counts, not abundance counts).  
  
- **[S/High — fixes false precision on the most-used estimator path/risk:none]** Add a nonparametric bootstrap CI wrapper around loglinear() in coverage/estimators.py, which currently returns a bare float with zero variance — the exact estimator path used whenever JobScout has 3+ real sources.  
  
- **[S/Medium-High — the one macro sanity gate that exists is currently silently non-functional below national scope/risk:none]** Fix coverage/jolts.py's _series_id(): it accepts area/naics but always returns the constant national series JTU000000000000000JOL. Build real state/2022-NAICS series IDs and return 'skip' honestly for sub-state areas (BLS MSA-level JOLTS is 'research estimates' only, not an official series).  
  
- **[M/High — company-level coverage currently has no macro ceiling at all/risk:none]** Add a company-level macro gate using the free Census County Business Patterns API (establishment counts by county/NAICS, free registration key) mirroring jolts_gate()'s pass/fail pattern, as the company-count analogue of the job-count JOLTS gate.  
  
- **[S/High — the single biggest correctness risk in the pipeline: undetected source correlation silently inflates reported coverage/risk:none]** Before computing f1/f2/membership in coverage/benchmark.py, group known-correlated job sources (any that re-syndicate Google-for-Jobs/Indeed content) into single meta-sources via an explicit independence declaration, mirroring the GOOD/BAD-pair docstring already in coverage/registry_coverage.py.  
  
- **[M/High — a single global coverage number is provably wrong for at least some strata; stratified numbers are defensible/risk:none]** Stratify capture-recapture (cov_cr, chao2, jackknife) per SOC/industry group, extending the per_soc pattern already built for the reference-proxy leg in coverage/reference.py, to address heterogeneous catchability between common and niche titles.  
  
- **[M/Medium — nearly free given existing jsonl-append infra; a second, non-parametric line of evidence/risk:none]** Reuse coverage/registry_history.py's rising/plateau/dry loop_signal machinery (currently company-registry-scoped) for job-level per-run accumulation curves too, as a cheap assumption-light convergence check.  
  
- **[S/High — this is what users actually see; prevents shipping a misleadingly precise, trust-eroding number/risk:none]** Surface coverage in the GUI as a CI-banded, source-count-qualified badge (never a bare percentage), with an honest 'cannot certify — need >=2 independent sources' fallback matching the existing CoverageEstimate.defined/nan behavior in coverage/registry_coverage.py.  
  
- **[S (recurring)/Medium — capture-recapture assumes perfect capture-history recording; resolve.py's threshold-85 rapidfuzz matching is the likeliest place that assumption fails/risk:none]** Periodically hand-audit a small sample (~50) of coverage/resolve.py's fuzzy-match clusters for false-merge/false-split rate and fold the estimated linkage-error rate into the reported CI, following the entity-linkage discipline in Beresewicz et al. 2021's job-vacancy DSE work.
